Dr. Martin Luther King Jr

The man who fought for racial equality

Here is a timeline of MLK's life:

  • 1929 - Born in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • 1944 - King begins his freshman year at Morehouse College.
  • 1948 - King receives his bachelor of arts degree in sociology.
  • 1953 - King marries Coretta Scott in Alabama.
  • 1955 - King becomes the president of the Montgomery Improvement Association.
  • 1957 - King attends the independence celebrations of the new nation of Ghana in West Africa and meets with Prime Minister Kwame Nkrumah.
  • 1958 - King publishes his first book, Stride Toward Freedom: The Montgomery Story.
  • 1959 - King embarks on a month-long visit to India where he meets with Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru and many of Ghandhi's followers.
  • 1960 - King meets with presidential candidate John F. Kennedy.
  • 1961 - King meets with President JFK and urges him to issue a second Emancipation Proclamation to elimiante racial segragation.
  • 1963 - U.S. attorney General Robert Kennedy authorizes the FBI to wiretap King's home phone
  • 1964 - King meets Malcolm X in Washington D.C.; also receives the Nobel Peace Prize in Oslo, Norway.
  • 1965 - King publicly opposes the Vietnam War at a mass rally.
  • 1967 - King publicly reveals his plan to organize a mass civil disobedince campaign to force the government to end poverty.
  • 1968 - King is shot and killed while standing on the balcony of the Lorraine Motel in Mephis.
